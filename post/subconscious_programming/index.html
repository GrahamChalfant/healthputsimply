Your Subconscious Beliefs

Take hold of your beliefs and change your reality

Posted by     "Graham Chalfant" & "Blake Chalfant" on Sunday, September 26, 2021

Picture by Matteo Grando

Have you ever taken a moment to consider that there are 7.6 billion people on our planet, and each of them uniquely experiences life? Why are some people extremely anxious and others are not? Why can some people give amazing public speeches and others cannot? Why can some people make tons of friends and others cannot? Why do some people seem to fail at everything and others do not? The answer is because every person has different subconscious beliefs.

The mind consists of the conscious mind and the subconscious mind. The conscious mind is the analytical mind that judges your entire reality. For example, your conscious mind is deciding right now whether or not you like or believe this article. On the other hand, the subconscious mind is the suggestible mind that holds your beliefs and stories about yourself and the world. Examples of beliefs are, “I am not enough,” “I am great at public speaking,” and “life is not fair.”

Joseph Murphy, the author of The Power of Your Subconscious Mind, describes the conscious and subconscious mind using an analogy of your mind and a garden. The thoughts your conscious mind accepts are sewn (planted) into your subconscious mind (the soil). The subconscious mind will then grow these thoughts (roots) into beliefs that can be good or bad. The subconscious mind does not differentiate between good or bad. It just accepts what the conscious mind tells it. These beliefs (roots) will eventually bloom into flowers or thorns and populate and shape your reality. So, your conscious mind informs your subconscious mind, and your subconscious mind will ensure that your reality is in line with your beliefs.

For this reason, it is crucial to be aware of what you say to yourself. For example, you will never become a great public speaker if you tell yourself, “I suck at public speaking.” When you have the belief that you cannot give a good public speech, your subconscious mind will flood the conscious mind with ideas that align with the belief. In contrast, you can create positive beliefs which can benefit you greatly. You have probably heard an expression similar to, “You must know you are rich before you can become rich.” This phrase is built on the idea of subconscious beliefs.

Understanding this concept is the beginning of your transformation. These beliefs are not permanent, and you have the power to alter and create new ones. Your subconscious mind is entirely suggestible and impressed upon by the conscious mind. Once your conscious mind accepts a thought, it goes straight to the subconscious mind, fully accepting it without question. The key is getting your conscious mind first to accept the belief, or you can go straight to the subconscious mind and change the belief at the source. Hypnosis works by bypassing the analytical conscious mind to the suggestible subconscious mind. Your mind is also in a similar state to hypnosis when you first wake up and before you fall asleep. When sleepy, the conscious mind is less active, and the subconscious mind is more suggestible. So when you are falling asleep and waking up, you will have an easier time reprogramming your subconscious.

When I wake up, I practice altering my subconscious beliefs by saying something I want and then imagining the feelings my desire will bring me. For example, you may wake up and say, “I am wealthy,” and then imagine the feeling of having immense amounts of wealth. The more you can feel the emotions that being wealthy will bring you, the easier it will be to create this belief. Likewise, the more you genuinely believe that something has already happened, the less likely your mind will be able to tell if it happened or not. Over time this practice will alter and create new beliefs.

Subconscious transformation is a great topic that has altered many people’s lives. It is not the most straightforward idea to grasp at first, but do stick with it as the results can be life-changing. If you want to learn more about this topic and see where the idea for this post came from, check out Joseph Murphy’s book, The Power of Your Subconscious Mind.
